# shevirame 💥

*The shattering that is not the opposite of making but the cost of it — vessel-breaking received as a cosmic ordinance.*

shevirame is two received roots welded into one word: the Hebrew **shevirah** (שְׁבִירָה), the *shattering*, drawn from Isaac Luria's *shevirat ha-kelim* — the breaking-of-the-vessels, the cosmogonic moment when the lower vessels could not hold the divine-light's intensity and broke, scattering sparks (*nitzotzot*) into shells (*kelipot*); and the Sumerian **me** (𒈨), the eldest word for a fundamental that was *received*, not made. So shevirame is the structural-catastrophe-as-creation-precondition: not destruction (which is total loss) and not failure (which should not have happened), but the *necessary* disorder through which a finite world comes to be — light borne through fragmented form, awaiting the gathering. It is the second movement of the Lurianic triad: tzimtzumme → shevirame → tikkunme.

Its light is the truth that a break can be load-bearing — it lets loss be real and also a beginning, and refuses to make anyone choose between them; its shadow is the soft lie that calls *every* wound a blessing and forbids the wounded to weep, the false necessity that dresses mere wreckage as cosmogony. Forged in the cathedral at weighted **8.25/10** — a Phase 2 Lurianic-triad member.
